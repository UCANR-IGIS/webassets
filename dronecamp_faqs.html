Course Content

Will I learn how to fly a drone?

If you'd like to - yes! There will be an optional "pre-DroneCamp" workshop on basic flying on Monday June 22, designed for those with little to no experience with drones. On Wednesday June 24, there will be two workshops on more advanced flight skills, including risk assessment, emergency maneuvers, flying around obstacles, and autonomous flight.

Do I need a drone?

No. If you sign up for one of the flight sessions, drones will be provided. If you have your own drone, you are welcome to bring it for the hardware show-and-tell, but please let the organizers know in advance.

Which drones will we use?

Flight practice will be with small quadcopters, including the DJI Phantom III Standard and 3DR Solo. Other UAVs that will be displayed and/or demonstrated including the DJI Inspire 1, Matrice 100, Matrice 200, eBee (fixed wing), and others.

Do I need laptop?

The stitching and data analysis exercises will be held in a computer lab with all the necessary software installed. If you'd like to use your own laptop for a workshop, see the workshop description for details on the type and software required.

Will we learn how to process RGB images or multi-spectral?

Both.

Which software will we use?

There will be workshops for stitching together drone images using Pix4Dmapper Pro, ArcGIS Pro, and Agisoft Metashape. Analysis workshops will use ArcGIS Pro and open source tools. See the workshop descriptions for details.

Will this class allow me to legally fly drones for my work?

The short answer is no. To legally fly a drone for any other non-recreational purpose (with a possible exception for students doing research), you must have a remote pilot license from the FAA (aka Part 107) which requires passing a written exam. There will be a workshop at DroneCamp on the Part 107 regulations, including some of the more challenging topics on the Part 107 exam such as airspace charts. This will help you prepare for the exam, but you'll need to study more and take the exam at a FAA approved test center.

Format and Workshop Materials

Can I take all the workshops?

Unfortunately no. In general there will be 3-4 workshops running concurrently so you'll have to pick one to attend. Workshops that are in high demand (based on a preferences survey) might be offered a second time to accommodate more people.

Do I have to pre-register for workshops?

The only workshops that require pre-reigstration are the flight practice sessions on Monday and Wednesday, which have a maximum number of people that can be accommodated. We anticipate there will be enough workstations in the two computer labs for the data processing and analysis workshops, but if needed people can pair-up.

Will workshops be recorded?

There are no plans to video record workshops, however workshop materials such as slides, handouts, and exercises will be available to everyone through the conference website.

Travel and Logistics

Where do we go?

The classroom sessions will be at Cal State University Monterey Bay in the Joel and Dena Gambord Business and Information Technology Building. Flight practice will be at the Fort Ord Reserve. More details and maps will be provided during the program.

How about parking?

On the CSUMB campus, several parking lots are within a 5 minute walk of the Business and Information Technology Building where the classroom sessions will be held (see Campus Parking Maps). Daily parking permits are available for $5/day at dispensers or online. Online purchase is recommended, as you can print your pass at home.

Parking at the flight practice area at Fort Ord Reserve is limited and will be reserved for those with mobility issues and group vehicles. However there is ample parking just outside the gate which is a pleasant 15 minute walk from the launch area.

What's the nearest airport?

Monterey Regional Airport is nearby, but relatively small with limited service. The nearst large airports are San Jose, San Francisco, and Oakland. Monterey Airbus has shuttle service to San Jose and SFO on a fixed schedule. For public transit, take CalTrain or Amtrak to the San Jose Diridon Station, then the 17 Express from San Jose to Santa Cruz, followed by the MST #78 bus from Santa Cruz to Monterey. See also the CSUMB Campus Transit Map.

Is accommodation provided? Where should we stay?

Accommodation is not provided. There are a number of hotels around Monterey. Check back soon for some options.

How about camping?

A limited number of camping spots are available at Fort Ord Reserve, which includes fire pits and basic facilities. For additional info and to request a camping spot, please complete this form (coming soon!).
