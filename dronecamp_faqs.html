Course Requirements

Do I need a drone?

No. Drones will be provided. If you have your own drone, you are welcome to bring it for the equipment show-and-tell, but please let the organizers know in advance.

Do I need laptop? If so, which kind?

To complete the data processing and analysis excecises (Wednesday June 20), you will need a Windows laptop with at least 8Gb of RAM and at least 20GB hard drive space.

I only have a Mac laptop. Will that work?

There's slightly older version of Pix4D for Macintosh that still works. The exercise we'll be doing however is written for the latest version of Pix4D, which is only available for Windows. Also, there is no easy way to install ArcGIS Pro on a Mac. Anyone who doesn't have a suitable laptop is welcome to work with someone else.

Course Content

Will we learn how to fly a drone?

Yes! Flight practice is part of the curriculum. Flight procedures will include equipment preparation and checks, manual launching and landing, automomous flight, and emergency procedures. In addition, students will be asked to practice on a flight simulator.

Which drones will we use?

Flight practice will be small quadcopters, including the DJI Phantom III Standard and 3DR Solo. A variety of other quadcopters will be displayed and/or demonstrated, including a DJI Inspire 1, and Matrice 100.

Will we learn how to process RGB images or multi-spectral?

Both.

Which software will we use?

The main software covered include Pix4Dmapper Pro and ArcGIS. Temporary software licenses will be provided for anyone who needs one.

Will this class allow me to legally fly drones for my work?

The short answer is no. To legally fly a drone for work, research, or any other non-recreational purpose, you must have a remote pilot license from the FAA (aka Part 107). DroneCamp will introduce the FAA regulations and provide some tips for getting your remote pilot license, but this is not a Part 107 exam prep class.

Can we get continuing education credits for this course?

No, but we can provide a letter with a detailed description of the curricula if that would help.
